10 Daily Habits Of Righteous Muslim Children

✍️ By NurulBayt Editorial Team 📅 June 2026 6 min read

Raising righteous children is a journey that requires consistency, love, and guidance from the Quran and Sunnah. While grand gestures are important, it is the small, daily habits that truly shape a child's character and faith. Here are 10 simple, practical daily habits inspired by Islamic teachings to help raise righteous Muslim children.

1. Waking Up Early (Barakah in the Morning)

The Prophet Muhammad () made dua for his Ummah: "O Allah, bless my Ummah in their early mornings." (Sunan Ibn Majah). Teach your children the value of waking up early, especially for Fajr. Even if they don't pray Fajr yet, waking up early gives them time to prepare for the day, eat a healthy breakfast, and start their morning with peace and barakah (blessing).

2. Reciting Morning Adhkar

Start the day with the remembrance of Allah. Teach your children simple morning adhkar like "SubhanAllah wa bihamdihi" (100 times) or "Ayat-ul-Kursi". These short phrases protect them throughout the day and keep their hearts connected to Allah. Make it a family routine to recite these together after Fajr or before leaving for school.

سُبْحَانَ اللهِ وَبِحَمْدِهِ

Dhikr: "SubhanAllahi wa bihamdihi"

'Glory be to Allah and His is the praise.'
— (Sahih Muslim)

3. Starting the Day with Bismillah

Teach your children to say "Bismillah" (In the name of Allah) before starting any task — before eating, before studying, before leaving the house, and before playing. This simple habit acknowledges that all our actions are for the sake of Allah and invites His blessings into everything we do.

4. Helping Parents (Birr al-Walidayn)

Kindness to parents is one of the most emphasized virtues in Islam. Encourage your children to help with small household chores — setting the table, tidying their room, or helping a younger sibling. Praise them for their help and remind them that pleasing their parents is a way to please Allah.

5. Learning or Reciting Quran Daily

Make Quran a daily habit, even if it's just for 5-10 minutes. This could be reading a few verses, memorizing a short surah, or listening to a recitation. The goal is to build a lifelong connection with the Book of Allah. Celebrate their progress, no matter how small.

6. Smiling and Good Character (Akhlaq)

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "Smiling at your brother is charity." (Tirmidhi). Teach your children the importance of good manners — saying "please" and "thank you," sharing with others, and being kind to animals. Good character (Akhlaq) is the heaviest thing on the Scale of Deeds.

7. Giving Daily Sadaqah (Even a Date)

Teach children the joy of giving. It doesn't have to be money. A smile, a kind word, or helping someone are all forms of Sadaqah. If you give money, let them put a coin in a "Sadaqah jar" every day. This builds a generous heart and teaches them that Allah loves those who give.

8. Performing the 5 Daily Prayers on Time

As children grow, make Salah a non-negotiable part of their daily routine. Pray together as a family whenever possible. Teach them the importance of praying on time and the beauty of standing before Allah. Make the prayer area clean, peaceful, and inviting.

🕌 Salah Guide: Need help teaching prayer? Read our detailed guide on 7 Easy Ways To Teach Salah To Children.

9. Reading Before Sleeping

Replace screen time before bed with reading. Choose Islamic stories, Seerah books, or age-appropriate Islamic literature. Reading not only improves their vocabulary and focus but also fills their hearts with Islamic knowledge before they sleep.

10. Sleeping with Wudu and Evening Adhkar

Teach your children to perform wudu before going to bed and to recite the evening adhkar and the "3 Quls" (Surah Al-Ikhlas, Al-Falaq, and An-Nas). The Prophet (ﷺ) taught us that sleeping in a state of purity is a blessed act. This habit protects them during the night and ensures they wake up in a state of barakah.

Final Thoughts

Building these habits takes time and patience. Don't try to implement all 10 at once. Start with 2-3 habits that resonate most with your family's current routine. Be consistent, praise their efforts, and make lots of dua for their righteousness. Remember, you are planting seeds that will grow into a strong, faith-filled life, Insha'Allah.

Frequently Asked Questions

How can I get my child to wake up for Fajr?+
Start by waking them up gently with a kind voice. Make the morning routine pleasant — perhaps with a favorite breakfast or a short family dhikr session. Avoid harshness or anger. Consistency and positive reinforcement are key.
What if my child refuses to do these habits?+
Don't force them. Instead, model the behavior yourself. Children learn by observation. Make the habits fun and rewarding. Praise their small efforts and make dua for their hearts to soften.
How long does it take to build a new habit?+
Research suggests it takes about 21 to 66 days to form a new habit. Be patient and consistent. Start with small, manageable steps and gradually build up.
