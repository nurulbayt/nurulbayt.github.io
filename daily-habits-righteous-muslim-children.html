10 Daily Habits Of Righteous Muslim Children

✍️ By NurulBayt Editorial Team 📅 June 2026 ⏱ 6 min read

Raising righteous children is the ultimate goal of every Muslim parent. But righteousness isn't built overnight — it's built through small, consistent daily habits rooted in the Quran and Sunnah. Here are 10 powerful daily habits that will help your children grow into confident, faithful, and loving Muslims.

1. Waking Up with Fajr and Morning Adhkar

The day begins with Allah. Teach your children to wake up for Fajr, even if they just pray two rak'ahs with you. After Fajr, recite the morning adhkar together. This sets a spiritual tone for the entire day and protects them from Shaytan.

Start small — even one or two short duas from the Sunnah are enough for young children. Consistency is more important than quantity.

2. Starting the Day with Bismillah

Teach your children to say "Bismillah" before everything — before eating, before drinking, before leaving the house, before starting homework. This simple habit connects every action to Allah and brings barakah (blessing) into their day.

💡 Practical Tip:

Make it a game! See who can remember to say Bismillah first before meals. Praise them when they remember, and gently remind them when they forget.

3. Being Kind and Respectful to Parents

Allah places immense importance on respecting parents. Teach your children to speak gently, listen attentively, and help their parents without being asked. The Prophet ﷺ said: "Paradise lies under the feet of your mother." (Sunan an-Nasa'i).

Model this behavior yourself. When your children see you treating your own parents with love and respect, they will naturally follow your example.

4. Learning and Reciting Quran Daily

The Quran is the speech of Allah. Make it a daily habit for your children to read, listen to, or memorize at least a few verses every day. Even 5-10 minutes of Quran connection can transform their hearts.

📖 Related: For practical ideas on making Quran fun, read our guide on Making Quran Learning Enjoyable For Kids.

5. Praying Salah on Time

Salah is the pillar of Islam. Teach your children to pray on time, not just when it's convenient. When the Adhan calls, everything else should pause. This teaches them discipline and prioritizes their relationship with Allah above worldly distractions.

6. Sharing and Being Generous

Islam teaches us to be generous. Encourage your children to share their toys, food, and time with others. Involve them in giving sadaqah (charity), even if it's just a few coins. This builds empathy and detaches them from materialism.

7. Speaking the Truth and Being Honest

The Prophet ﷺ was known as Al-Sadiq (The Truthful) even before prophethood. Teach your children that honesty is always the best policy, even when it's difficult. Praise them when they tell the truth, especially when it would have been easier to lie.

8. Helping Others in the Home

The Prophet used to help his family with household chores. Teach your children that helping at home is a form of sadaqah. Whether it's setting the table, cleaning their room, or helping a sibling, these small acts build character and responsibility.

9. Making Dua Before Sleeping

End the day with Allah. Teach your children the bedtime adhkar and encourage them to make personal duas before sleeping. This is a beautiful time when duas are accepted, and it helps them fall asleep with peace in their hearts.

بِاسْمِكَ اللَّهُمَّ أَمُوتُ وَأَحْيَا

Bedtime Dua: "Bismika Allahumma amutu wa ahya."

'In Your name, O Allah, I die and I live.'
— (Sahih Bukhari)

10. Reflecting and Saying Alhamdulillah

Teach your children to reflect on their day before sleeping. What did they do well? What can they improve? And most importantly, say "Alhamdulillah" for every blessing. Gratitude is the key to happiness in this life and the next.

Final Thoughts

Raising righteous children is a journey of love, patience, and consistency. You don't have to implement all 10 habits at once. Pick one or two to focus on this week, and gradually build from there. Remember, every small effort you make is an investment in your child's akhirah. May Allah make our children the coolness of our eyes. Ameen.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I get my child to start these habits? +
Start small and be consistent. Pick one habit to focus on for a week. Model the behavior yourself, make it fun, and praise their efforts. Don't overwhelm them with too many changes at once.
What if my child refuses to follow these habits? +
Stay calm and patient. Understand why they're resisting. Make the habit more appealing, involve them in the process, and most importantly, make dua for their guidance. Never force or shame them.
At what age should I start teaching these daily habits? +
You can start introducing simple habits like saying Bismillah and Alhamdulillah from toddlerhood. More structured habits like Salah and Quran recitation can begin around age 7, as recommended by the Prophet ﷺ.
