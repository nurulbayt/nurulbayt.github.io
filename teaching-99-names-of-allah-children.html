Teaching Children the 99 Names of Allah

✍️ By NurulBayt Editorial Team 📅 July 2026 ⏱ 6 min read

Allah has 99 beautiful names, known as Asma-ul-Husna. Each name reveals a different aspect of His perfection, mercy, and power. Teaching these names to our children is not just about memorization; it is about helping them build a deep, personal relationship with their Creator. When a child knows that Allah is Al-Wadud (The Loving), they feel safe. When they know He is Al-Ghaffar (The Forgiving), they feel hope. Here are fun, simple, and practical ways to teach your children the 99 Names of Allah.

1. Start with the Names They Already Know

Children are often familiar with "Allah" and "Rabb" before they even understand what the words mean. Start there. Then, introduce the most common and comforting names: Ar-Rahman (The Most Merciful), Ar-Rahim (The Especially Merciful), and Al-Malik (The King). Explain them in simple terms: "Ar-Rahman means Allah is so merciful that He gives us everything we need, even before we ask."

2. Connect the Names to Their Daily Lives

The best way to teach abstract concepts is to make them concrete. Connect Allah's names to your child's everyday experiences.

  • Al-Wadud (The Loving): When you hug them, say, "Allah is Al-Wadud. He loves you even more than I do."
  • Al-Ghaffar (The Forgiving): When they make a mistake and apologize, say, "Allah is Al-Ghaffar. He forgives you when you say sorry."
  • Ar-Razzaq (The Provider): When eating a meal, say, "Allah is Ar-Razzaq. He provided this delicious food for us."
  • As-Salam (The Source of Peace): When they feel scared at night, say, "Allah is As-Salam. He keeps us safe and gives our hearts peace."

3. Use Visual Aids and Crafts

Children are visual learners. Create a beautiful "Asma-ul-Husna" wall in their room or the play area. You can buy pre-made posters, or better yet, make them together! Write each name in Arabic, English, and Urdu/your native language. Let them decorate the borders with their favorite colors.

💡 Practical Tip:

Create a set of flashcards. On one side, write the name in Arabic; on the other, write the meaning and a simple drawing representing it (e.g., a sun for An-Noor, The Light). Review a few cards together every day after breakfast.

4. Implement a "Name of the Week" Routine

Trying to teach all 99 names at once can be overwhelming. Instead, focus on one name per week. Every Monday, introduce a new name. Throughout the week, look for opportunities to mention it. By the end of the week, your child will have a deep understanding of that specific attribute of Allah. In 99 weeks (less than 2 years), they will have learned all the beautiful names!

5. Incorporate the Names into Daily Dua

Dua is the perfect time to use the names of Allah. Teach your children to call upon Allah by the name that fits their need. If they are worried about a test, teach them to say: "Ya Alim (The All-Knowing), help me remember what I studied." If they are feeling sad, teach them: "Ya Jabbar (The Mender of Hearts), please make my heart happy." This teaches them that Allah is not just a distant creator, but a close, responsive friend.

6. Tell Stories from the Quran and Sunnah

The Quran and the life of the Prophet Muhammad (ﷺ) are full of stories that highlight Allah's names. When teaching Al-Fattah (The Opener), tell the story of how Allah opened the sea for Prophet Musa (AS). When teaching Al-Hafiz (The Protector), tell the story of the cave of Thawr. Stories make the names come alive and help children remember them for a lifetime.

إِنَّ لِلَّهِ تِسْعَةً وَتِسْعِينَ اسْمًا، مِائَةً إِلَّا وَاحِدًا، مَنْ أَحْصَاهَا دَخَلَ الْجَنَّةَ

Hadith: "Inna lillahi tis'atan wa tis'ina isman, mi'atan illa wahidan, man ahsaha dakhala al-jannah."

'Verily, Allah has 99 names, 100 minus one. Whoever memorizes them (and acts upon them) will enter Paradise.'
— (Sahih Bukhari)

7. Play Interactive Games

Learning should be fun! Play games like "Guess the Name": Give them a scenario ("I gave you your toy back after you lost it. Which name of Allah does this remind you of?" -> Al-Wahhab, The Giver). Or play "Name Bingo" with family members. The more enjoyable the process, the more they will want to learn.

Final Thoughts

Teaching the 99 Names of Allah is a beautiful journey that will shape your child's worldview. When they know Allah's names, they will know how to turn to Him in every situation. Be patient, make it fun, and most importantly, make dua that Allah opens their heart to His beautiful names and attributes.

Frequently Asked Questions

At what age should I start teaching my child the 99 names?+
You can start introducing simple names like Ar-Rahman and Al-Wadud as early as age 2 or 3 through daily conversation. Formal memorization can begin around age 5 or 6, depending on the child's interest and ability.
Does my child need to memorize the Arabic names perfectly?+
While memorizing the Arabic names is a great goal and carries reward, the primary objective is for them to understand the meaning and connect with Allah. Focus on the meaning first; the Arabic pronunciation will follow with consistent exposure.
How can I make learning the names less boring for my child?+
Avoid long lectures. Use stories, crafts, games, and daily life connections. Keep sessions short (5-10 minutes) and always end on a positive, encouraging note.
